FocalGaussian: Text-Driven 3D Human Generation with Focal Paradigm

Our FocalGaussian generates high fidelity and 3D-consistent human models with delicate human parts like hands from text prompts.

Abstract

Text-driven 3D human generation significantly reduces manual labor for professionals and enables non-professionals to create 3D assets, facilitating applications across various fields, such as digital games, advertising, and films.

Conventional methods usually follow the paradigm of optimizing 3D representations such as neural radiance field and 3D Gaussian by distilling a diffusion model. However, existing methods struggle to generate delicate human body parts and usually suffer from the Janus (multi-face) problem, primarily due to the ignorance of imposing stable local topology control and precise viewpoint control.

Our key idea is to focus on the critical components of the human body parts to impose a more precise control while optimizing the 3D model. Following this, we propose FocalGaussian. Specifically, to eliminate the local blurry problem, we propose a focal depth loss that recovers delicate human body parts by aligning the depth of local body parts in the 3D human model and SMPL-X. Moreover, to address the Janus problem, we propose a focal view-dependent prompt that emphasizes and imposes view direction control on the keywords of local body parts.

Extensive experiments demonstrate the superiority of our FocalGaussian across a variety of prompts. Critically, our generated 3D humans accurately capture complex features of human body parts, particularly the hands.

Motivation

Motivation of our proposed FocalGaussian. (a) T2I models struggle to synthesize delicate body parts like hands. (b) Employing prompt weighting on keywords helps match the generated content and text prompt. (c) Adding view direction while generating local body parts provides a more precise view control. \( \mathcal{T} \) is the text prompt "a Joker" in this case.

Method

Overview of our FocalGaussian. Given a text prompt \( \mathcal{T} \), we create a realistic 3D avatar with delicate body parts like hands and face that matches \( \mathcal{T} \). The key idea of FocalGaussian is to focus on the key components of the human body. We first initialize a 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) model with a SMPL-X model. During training, to recover delicate human parts, we design focal depth loss (c.f. Sec. 3.1) to align the visible hand depth of SMPL-X and 3DGS. Moreover, to address the Janus (multi-face) problem, we design a focal view-dependent prompt Score Distillation Sampling loss (c.f. Sec. 3.2) that uses our designed prompts \( \mathcal{T}_{LV} \) to up-weight the keywords of body parts and to impose finer view control on a fixed diffusion model.

Effects of Focal Depth Loss

We adjust the loss ratio \( \lambda_1 \) of the focal depth loss to show its effectiveness on recovering hands. We gradually increase the loss ratio from 0.5 to 50000, shown from left to right. The model trained with \( \lambda_1 \leq 50 \) fails to recover the hands with clear fingers, while the model trained with \( \lambda_1 \geq 500.0 \) successfully recovers the hands.
